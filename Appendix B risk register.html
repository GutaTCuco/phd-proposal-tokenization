Appendix B: Risk Register (PMI Format)

Description: This Risk Register provides a systematic overview of the most critical risks identified for the real estate tokenization project in Portugal. Each risk is categorized, assessed for probability and impact, and assigned a specific response strategy with corresponding mitigation actions. This document serves as a key project management tool for proactively monitoring and controlling threats to the project's success.

Risk ID Risk Description Category P (1-5) I (1-5) Score (PxI) Response Strategy Specific Mitigation Actions Owner
R-01 Liquidity Paradox: The secondary market for tokens fails to achieve sufficient liquidity, preventing investors from easily exiting their positions and undermining a core value proposition. Market 4 5 20 Mitigate 1. Act as an initial market maker to guarantee baseline liquidity.
2. Incentivize external market makers.
3. Seek listings on regulated DLT marketplaces.		 Project Lead
R-02 Unfavorable Tax Regime: The Portuguese tax law treats security tokens as traditional securities, applying a 28% capital gains tax regardless of the holding period, which eliminates a key incentive for retail crypto investors. Legal & Regulatory 5 4 20 Accept & Mitigate 1. Radical transparency in all investor communications regarding the applicable tax regime.
2. Focus the value proposition on rental income and fractional access, not tax benefits.		 Legal & Comms
R-03 Operational Mismanagement: The physical real estate asset is poorly managed (e.g., lack of maintenance, unpaid taxes), leading to value erosion and legal liabilities, as demonstrated by the RealT case. Operational 3 5 15 Mitigate 1. Outsource property management to a professional, independent, and reputable local firm.
2. Conduct regular and independent physical and financial audits.		 Operations
R-04 Land Registry Barrier: The inability to legally register fractional ownership directly on-chain necessitates the use of a Special Purpose Vehicle (SPV), adding cost, complexity, and agency risks. Legal & Regulatory 5 3 15 Mitigate 1. Use a legally sound SPV structure (e.g., Lda.).
2. Ensure SPV legal documents are transparently linked to the token via IPFS hash.
3. Implement a hybrid governance model to mitigate agency risk.		 Legal Team
R-05 Smart Contract Vulnerability: A bug or exploit in the complex ERC-3643 smart contract suite leads to a permanent and irreversible loss of investor funds. Technical 2 5 10 Mitigate 1. Commission multiple independent security audits from top-tier firms.
2. Implement a public bug bounty program before mainnet launch.
3. Adhere to established security standards.		 Tech Lead
R-06 Low Financial/Digital Literacy: The target audience (retail investors) may lack the necessary knowledge to understand the product's risks and mechanics, leading to low adoption or poor investment decisions. Market 4 2 8 Mitigate 1. Integrate a comprehensive educational hub into the platform.
2. Simplify the User Interface (UI/UX) to abstract technical complexity.
3. Host regular webinars and Q&A sessions.		 Product & Marketing
R-07 ESG Concerns: The project faces scrutiny regarding the energy consumption of the chosen L2 network, its potential social impact on local housing markets (gentrification), and data privacy issues related to KYC. ESG & Reputational 3 2 6 Mitigate 1. Choose a Proof-of-Stake (PoS) L2 for minimal energy use.
2. Partner with municipalities on affordable housing projects.
3. Ensure full GDPR compliance and data minimization.		 Project Lead
