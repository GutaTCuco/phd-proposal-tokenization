Appendix B: Risk Register

Part 1: Simulated Academic Pilot

  ID Risk Description Impact Likelihood Risk Level Mitigation Strategy
0 B.1.1 Simulation Fidelity Risk. The simulated market model (e.g., asset price fluctuations, secondary market liquidity) does not accurately represent the dynamics of a real market, leading to conclusions with low external validity. High (on research validity) Medium High
  • Utilize multiple data sources (oracles) for the price model, basing it on real market indices.
  • Model liquidity based on empirical data from other fractional investment platforms.
  • Explicitly state this limitation in the thesis.
1 B.1.2 Behavioral Gap Risk (Hawthorne Effect). Participants act differently than they would in a real-world scenario (e.g., taking excessive risks) because they are aware they are in an experiment and are not using real money. High (on research validity) High High
  • Design the simulation with realistic constraints (e.g., transaction costs, initial capital limits).
  • Use pre- and post-pilot surveys to gauge risk perception.
  • Acknowledge the potential behavioral gap as a core limitation in the research findings.
2 B.1.3 Technical & Operational Risk. The pilot platform or its underlying smart contracts contain bugs, or the external data oracles providing market data fail, leading to simulation downtime or corrupted data. Medium (on pilot execution) Medium Medium
  • Conduct thorough testing of the platform and smart contracts on a testnet before the pilot.
  • Implement redundant oracle sources and a monitoring system for data feed integrity.
  • Have a clear communication plan for participants in case of technical issues.
3 B.1.4 Data Privacy & Ethical Risk. The collection, storage, and analysis of participant data (including KYC and survey responses) do not comply with GDPR, or ethical standards for academic research are not fully met. High (on legal and ethical compliance) Low Medium
  • Develop a comprehensive Data Management Plan.
  • Anonymize all research data prior to analysis.
  • Ensure the study protocol is approved by an institutional Ethics Committee.
  • Provide participants with a clear informed consent form.

Part 2: Future Commercial Implementation

Note: This register contains the primary risks identified for a potential commercial-scale implementation with real assets.

  ID Risk Description Impact Likelihood Risk Level Mitigation Strategy
0 B.2.1 Regulatory Ambiguity. Uncertainty in the tax treatment of security tokens and legal barriers at the Land Registry. High High High Use of an SPV legal structure; transparent communication with users about tax obligations.
1 B.2.2 Smart Contract Vulnerability. A bug or exploit in the on-chain contracts could lead to loss of funds or functionality. Critical Low Medium Multiple independent security audits of the smart contract code; bug bounty program.
2 B.2.3 Low Secondary Market Liquidity. Inability for investors to sell their tokens quickly at a fair price, undermining a key value proposition. High High High Platform acts as an initial market maker; partnerships with secondary market providers.
3 B.2.4 Operational Failure (Off-Chain). Mismanagement of the physical property (e.g., maintenance, rent collection) compromises the asset's value. High Medium High Selection of a reputable and independent property manager; establishment of a maintenance reserve fund.
4 B.2.5 Negative ESG Impact. High energy consumption from the blockchain or contribution to local housing gentrification. Medium Medium Medium Use of an energy-efficient Proof-of-Stake (PoS) Layer-2; partnerships with municipalities to ensure alignment with housing goals.
